My hobbies

1. Knapping stone tools: The first time I knapped was in 2004 in a class called Experimental Archaeology. I had lots of beginner's luck and managed to come home with an obsidian acheulian handax that had too much to eat. It wasn't until graduate school that I earnestly began to flint knap, thanks to Steve Shackley. From 2007 to 2010, I improved my flint knapping skills with obsidian and occasionally basalt and dacite. Recently, thanks to Bruce Bradley, I started knapping chert. I still have a long way to go to mastery, but I am enjoying the journey. Knapping stone tools is a wonderful way to overcome writer's block!

3. Art: I'm an amateur at art. In high school, I was a teen apprentice and teaching assistant at youth summer camp at the Museum of Contemporary Art in Chicago. Even in high school, my love for archaeology was apparent because I sculpted a Catal Hoyuk head for exhibition at the Museum of Contemporary Art Chicago as part of the teen apprentice program. My favorite type of art is sculpture, but I am working on my painting skills at the moment. In 2014, I drew and painted anthropomorphic animals for donors of a library project for Pomacocha.
